WHAT IS BRANDING ?

A brand identity is a major part of the marketing plan of a business, and it refers to the set of tools or elements used by a company to create a brand image. A brand image is a customer's perception of the brand, consisting of various associations related to it and memories about interacting with it. A brand's identity and its elements stem from a companyâ€™s mission, brand value proposition, long-term goals, competitive position on the market, and relevance to the values and interests of the target audience. These factors have a foundational nature and, in the branding process, describe what a company wants to communicate.

BRANDING STRATEGIES

As mentioned before, branding is not only going to help a brand differentiate from the competition, but it also gives the audience a compelling reason to choose a particular brand.

There are different strategies that a brand can use to engage its audience and resonate with who they are, but generally, the aim of every strategy is to make the audience remember the business, and the best way to do this is to give them a reason why they should remember the brand.

What are the most important strategies?

There are many brand strategies, but I am going to focus on four important ones :
  • Experiential Branding
  • Activist Branding
  • Personality Branding
  • Personality Branding

Experiential Branding

Experiential branding is all about actively designing sensory engagements with the audience, and these sensory engagements are usually very unique. The advantage of these sensory engagements is that they are easily remembered, making it easier for the audience to recall the brand.

Activist Branding

This branding strategy is all about brands being more vocal about issues within society or political issues. There is no room for error in this strategy, which is why the tiniest mistake can lead to a viral scandal.

Personality Branding

This strategy is a lot more simplistic and applicable than the previous ones. There is nothing really technical about this approach; it relies on a good definition of the audience and understanding them on an intimate level and the type of communication that they would respond to and give, then giving them that communication and eventually providing them with whatever resonates with them. The beauty of this strategy is that it is not complicated; it's effective, efficient, and economical as well, and if it's done right, it can really engage the audience with who they are and what they want, really connect with them on an emotional level, and capture their hearts as well.

Distinctive asset branding:

Being distinctive is key for any brand out there; this will help the audience remember a brand when they are making their buying decision. The reason why distinctive brand assets are effective is that they create memory structures, and the more memory structures that we have for a brand, the better chance we have of finding our way back to that brand experience or remembering that brand during the buying decision. Therefore, a brand should consider creating as many distinctive assets as possible and putting them on the market, on social media platforms, on websites, or at any other single touchpoint to give their audience the best possible chance of remembering their brand.

Social Media Content

In an age that is governed by social media, every brand has to be very indulgent and give serious attention to their social media accounts. Social media is the most important medium between a brand and their audiences these days, which means any business or organization should hire a highly professional team to manage their social media accounts in order to get the best results out of them. A brand's social media team should at least be composed of a community manager, a content specialist, a social media manager, and a digital marketer; each of them will be assigned a different task that will take their audiences through a unique digital brand experience. A good brand experience can help brands build a close relationship with their audiences that will eventually advocate for them and take marketing actions on their behalf.
